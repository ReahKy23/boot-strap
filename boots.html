Is This the End of Media Literacy?

Media literacy is a skillset that is lacking in many of today’s social media users. Media literacy is the ability to navigate through the large and ever-growing landscape that is the internet consciously. Media Literacy allows for a user to be mindful of various subconscious tactics that digital companies exploit to boost viewership and usage from their consumers. Being able to distinguish what videos are genuine inquiries or intentionally rage baiting is one example in which media literacy is valuable.

The End of the Creative

Social media is not for the “creative”. With such dependency on “the collective” there is no space for individuality. Interestingly enough, appreciation for people and art styles that went outside the “box” only obtained their flowers after the original creator was driven off the app of the artistic space. A lot of the later “appreciation” is because so many people want to “create” or “establish” a new trend, but in retrospect you can tell they never did because they wouldn’t have the backbone to be the only person straying from the narrative. Individuality even in the face of conformity is what makes a person “different” or an innovator.” A lot of the later appreciation tends to just eb co-opting in already established cultural spaces.
